Must-Try Dishes

The Food

Two dishes you absolutely must try on the Amalfi Coast — both unique to this region and impossible to replicate anywhere else in the world.

Scialatielli
The Amalfi Coast's own pasta, born in Positano in 1978

A thick, short, chewy fresh pasta made with flour, milk, basil and pecorino — so it already has flavor before any sauce is added. Almost always served with seafood: mixed frutti di mare, clams in white wine, or the splurge version with lobster. The robust texture holds up beautifully to briny, fresh seafood sauces. Order it everywhere you see it.

Delizia al Limone
The lemon dessert that tastes like the Amalfi Coast itself

A dome-shaped individual cake filled with lemon pastry cream, soaked in lemon syrup, and covered in a cloud of lemon chantilly. The lemons here (sfusato amalfitano) are a special local variety — larger, sweeter, and more fragrant than any lemon you've tasted before. This dessert is bright, creamy, refreshing, and utterly unforgettable in the July heat.

"First, we eat."

Before You Go

Essential Tips

🛂
No ETIAS Needed

ETIAS doesn't launch until late 2026 — after your trip. All you need are valid US passports. Check they're valid through at least November 1, 2026 (3 months after departure).

🧳
Pack Light

The Amalfi Coast means hundreds of stairs. Heavy luggage is a nightmare. Pack light, and confirm baggage allowance — your Economy Basic tickets have restrictions.

🌡️
Beat the Heat

July is hot — especially in Pompeii and Naples. Schedule outdoor sightseeing for early morning and evening. Afternoon rest time is the Italian way for good reason!

🎟️
Book in Advance

Reserve Pompeii ruins entry, Capri ferry tickets, and Praiano restaurants well before you go. July is peak season and things sell out fast.

⛵
Take the Boat

Whenever possible on the Amalfi Coast, travel by boat rather than the coast road bus. Faster, more scenic, and avoids the notoriously congested SS163 highway.

🍋
Try Everything

Scialatielli, delizia al limone, limoncello, Neapolitan pizza, sfusato amalfitano lemons. These flavors are unique to this corner of Italy — eat adventurously!
